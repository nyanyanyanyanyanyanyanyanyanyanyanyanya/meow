XY022: PokÃ©mon: Mega Evolution Special
(ãƒã‚±ãƒƒãƒˆãƒ¢ãƒ³ã‚¹ã‚¿ãƒ¼XYç‰¹åˆ¥ç·¨ æœ€å¼·ãƒ¡ã‚¬ã‚·ãƒ³ã‚« ~Act I~)

The mega evolution special! The mega evolution specials are separated into four acts, and this is the first one. These episodes don't follow Ash and his friends, they follow a trainer named Alain instead. When the episode starts we're given a narration about mega evolution, which at this point, is still a very mysterious phenonemom being researched. We meet Alain who is battling an absol trainer, and both of their Pokemon mega evolve! It's so cool!


A new trainer who just started her journey overhears the battle, her name is Mairin, and she spies on the battle just in time to see both of their mega evolutions. Alain wins the fight, and when Mairin approaches, we learn a little about Alain. We learn that he's actually a former research assistant of Professor Sycamore's! We flash back to when Alain was still an assistant and Charizard was just a charmander, and he has a conversation with Professor Sycamore that ultimately results in him asking if he can go on a journey with charmander.


The nature of their conversation is important, it's about Professor Sycamore explaining what they know about mega evolution and how it works to their knowledge. He confirms a lot of little things about mega evolution, such as the power coming from the Pokemon, that a human is necessary to draw out the power of the bond, and speculation about the changing types/abilities happens during battle. He explains that mega evolution is such a big topic of his research because it, to his knowledge, only happens in Kalos so it's a giant topic of conversation when talking about Pokemon in general. It's Alain who suggests leaving and finding more samples - mega stones - to aid Professor Sycamore's research, and Sycamore encourages this, so long as Alain reports back to him.


The episode continues with a few more battles, Alain finding a new mega stone, and Mairin catching a Flabebe. It ends on Alain's loss to Siebold of the Elite 4.


The mega evolution specials are very polarizing, I think. They rely on the viewer to like Alain, and Alain himself is a controversial character, both in the context of Professor Sycamore and in the anime fandom as a whole. At this point, if it's not already obvious, Professor Sycamore's story, characterization, themes, ect have been changed a lot from the games, and Alain is a new addition to this. Elements such as Dexio, Sina, charizard, or even his friendship with Lysandre, have been removed and retrofitted into Alain instead.


I, personally, enjoy Alain, Mairin, ect for what they are. I enjoy Professor Sycamore as he's depicted but I do understand it's very different from what he was. I definitely do understand the disappointment fans of the games might feel though, given that yes, his story, characterization, themes, ect are all massively changed. On some level, it can appear as tone deaf to what he originally represented, and XY's story as a whole. I also think that in terms of adaptation, Sycamore and XY could do a lot worse... the anime's track record is a bit suspicious sometimes...


I'm getting a little offtopic! The battle animations are fine, and the battles were fun. Seeing Siebold was great, I really liked how Alain requested a special meal while sitting at the table like it were some secret exchange. We are really shown how mysterious mega evolution is, and when Alain remembers his conversations with Professor Sycamore, Professor Sycamore is depicted very kindly... Alain, who is quiet and distant, is shown in a different light in those flashbacks. You get the sense of how much the professor means to him, and how he keeps that to himself.


Much of the first act is spent introducing characters, concepts, and battling, but overall, this is a good start!

(ãƒãƒ£ãƒ³ãƒ”ã‚ªãƒ³ãƒ»ã‚«ãƒ«ãƒç™»å ´ï¼éœ§ã®ä¸­ã®ãƒ¡ã‚¬ã‚µãƒ¼ãƒŠã‚¤ãƒˆï¼ï¼)

Bonds of Evolutionmarks Professor Sycamore's first appearance in the anime since episode 10, if you don't include his flashback in the PokÃ©mon: Mega Evolution Special. This is also the first episode that the champion Diantha makes her appearance in the anime. Diantha is a favorite of mine and I find that this is an amazing introduction to her as a character. Ash and his friends are going to an exhibition match she's participating in, where they learn that she's not just the champion but also an accomplished actress. They meet Professor Sycamore who's there on business on his own. We find that he's a good "inside guy" to be able to meet the champion directly!

The episode is primarily about Diantha's relationship with Gardevoir, showing how much a bond matters between the trainer and their Pokemon, and displaying what a champion-level bond looks like. Diantha and her Gardevoir are so effortlessly in tune with one another, their relationship is on another level! Professor Sycamore's business actually has to do with Diantha's keystone... he'd like to borrow it for study, but she says no, because it's so important to her and Gardevoir. He tries to finesse his way into getting the keystone but is promptly told no! As he should be. Bonds are very important and intimate and he shouldn't just be so forward about intruding on them!

That being said, several battles take place over the course of the episode, including a climax against Team Rocket who tries to abduct Gardevoir. We learn more about Diantha and Gardevoir's relationship. It's very sweet, and Professor Sycamore is given more context about how much connection matters with mega evolution. All in all, I think this is a great episode, especially with Diantha as the focus! She is so poised, confident, and beautiful, and her relationship with Gardevoir is displayed so nicely. It really puts forward the bonds and connection aspects of mega evolution.

I think Professor Sycamore is fine for this episode. We're shown more of his more mischevious side when we see him sneaking backstage to meet Diantha directly, and how he tries(and fails) to charm Diantha's assistant in order to smooth things over for keystone access. The XY games mentions his eccentricities and how he's a ladies' man and this episode tries to illustrate that.
