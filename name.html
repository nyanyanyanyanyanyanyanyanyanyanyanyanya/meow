What's in a Name?

Names in Pokémon can be finicky. Professor Augustine Sycamore is one of the few characters in the franchise with both a first and surname, which makes him very special! Professor Sycamore is called his first name by multiple characters in the game, most notably the reporter Alexa, and the Champion Diantha. The etymology is mostly found in Latin and botanical sources. Let's start with his first name!

Augustine is from the Latin "Augustinus". That means venerable magnificant! "Augustus" can also be derived from this, which means sacred. Of course, the name also relates to the scholar, Saint Augustine of Hippo. St Augustine was a very influentical Christian philosopher, and he was actually known for his wild youth! The name suggests someone who finds meaning through experience first, and doctrine second. That's very fitting for Professor Sycamore, I think! Additionally, I think the name is very gentle and elegant, and I bet it appears lovely as a signature.

Of course, everybody knows that each professor in the franchise is usually named after a kind of tree... so let's dive into more about Sycamore trees! Sycamore trees are known for being tall, and their big canopies. Did you know that they're often planted in town squares or other gathering places specifically because they'e excellent for comunity spaces? For example, Central Park in New York City. Sycamore trees flourish along riverbanks, floodplains, and moist areas. Do you think it's a coincidence that Professor Sycamore is often seen in places with lots of water too, such as Coumarine and Couriway?

The ancient Eygptians considered sycamore trees sacred to Isis, the goddess of magic and wisdom, and representative of knowledge and rebirth. In Christian symbolism, they represent chriosity, learning, and the desire to see more. For example, the story of Zacchaeus! Zacchaeus climbs a sycamore tree so he can meet Jesus.

I feel like that they very specifically picked a tree that means 'the place where people come together to share knowledge'. The combination of Augustine+Sycamore suggests someone who creates special spaces through authentic experience.

Dr. Platane and Localization

His name, in original Japanese, is プラターヌ博士 or Dr. Platane. Platane is the French word for Plane tree. Did you know that plane trees and sycamore trees are closely related? They're both in the platanacaea family. Many languages kept his platane name. Why bring this up? Well, the english name 'Augustine' can also refer to Augustine Henry, a real life botanist who discovered a certain kind of plane trees. This really says to me that a lot of work went into the English localization.

I love how the name pattern shows a man who creates spaces wherever he goes. Plane trees and sycamore trees are often specifically chosen because they're so good at providing shelter. That's exactly what his character does: he welcomes the player character to the region, gives you two starter Pokémon, and goes out of his way to help the player character feel at home in Kalos. Which, by the start of XY, is a very new place for the player character indeed! They just moved to Vanivelle Town, after all. His name, be it プラターヌ博士 or Professor Sycamore, works great across many languages. Is this because the concept of a 'comfortable gathering place' is universal? Who can say!
